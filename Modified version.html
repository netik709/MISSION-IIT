Blog & Study Tips

How I Manage 8+ Hours of Daily Study

Posted on: October 1, 2025

Consistency is key. I wake up at 5 AM every day and follow the Pomodoro technique - 50 minutes of focused study followed by 10 minutes break. I divide my day into blocks for Maths, Physics, and Chemistry. Evening is reserved for solving previous year papers and revision.

Best Strategy for IOQM Preparation

Posted on: September 28, 2025

IOQM requires deep conceptual understanding and problem-solving skills. Focus on number theory, combinatorics, and geometry. Solve problems from Mathematical Circles and previous IOQM papers. Practice is everything - aim for at least 20 problems daily.

Overcoming Physics Problems in Irodov

Posted on: September 25, 2025

Irodov can be intimidating, but it's the best book for building problem-solving skills. Start with mechanics and work your way up. Don't skip steps in solutions - understanding the methodology is more important than getting the answer.

Why Mental Health Matters in JEE Preparation

Posted on: September 20, 2025

It's a marathon, not a sprint. Take breaks, exercise daily, and don't burn out. I make sure to play badminton for 30 minutes every evening. A healthy mind performs better than an exhausted one.

Q: How to approach rotational motion problems?

Subject: Physics | Asked by: Rahul

Answer: Start by identifying the axis of rotation and apply torque equations. Use energy conservation when possible - it often simplifies the problem significantly.

Q: Best method for integration of trigonometric functions?

Subject: Mathematics | Asked by: Priya

Answer: Learn the standard forms first. For complex problems, try substitution methods. Converting everything to sine and cosine often helps.

Q: How to remember organic chemistry reactions?

Subject: Chemistry | Asked by: Amit

Answer: Don't just memorize - understand the mechanism. Make flashcards and revise daily. NCERT examples are gold for JEE.
